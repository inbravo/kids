Q1Easy
The x-coordinate of every point lying on the y-axis is:
1
0
−1
Any real number
Hint: The y-axis is the vertical line where x = 0. Every point on it looks like (0, y) for some y — so the x-coordinate is always 0.
Q2Easy
The point where the x-axis and y-axis intersect is called the:
Unit point
Centre
Origin
Vertex
Hint: The intersection point of the two axes is the Origin, with coordinates (0, 0). It is the reference point for the entire Cartesian plane.
Q3Easy
The point (−3, 5) lies in which quadrant?
Quadrant I
Quadrant II
Quadrant III
Quadrant IV
Hint: x is negative, y is positive → Quadrant II (top-left). Remember: I (+,+), II (−,+), III (−,−), IV (+,−).
Q4Easy
The y-coordinate of every point lying on the x-axis is:
0
1
−1
Any real number
Hint: The x-axis is the horizontal line where y = 0. Every point on it looks like (x, 0) — so the y-coordinate is always 0.
Q5Easy
In which quadrants do both coordinates have the same sign (both positive or both negative)?
I and II
II and III
I and III
II and IV
Hint: Quadrant I: (+,+) — same sign ✓. Quadrant III: (−,−) — same sign ✓. In II and IV the signs are opposite.
Q6Easy
The point (4, −6) lies in which quadrant?
Quadrant I
Quadrant II
Quadrant III
Quadrant IV
Hint: x = 4 (positive), y = −6 (negative) → Quadrant IV (bottom-right).
Q7Easy
The Cartesian plane is divided into how many quadrants?
2
3
4
6
Hint: The two axes divide the plane into exactly 4 quadrants, numbered I to IV going anti-clockwise from the top-right.
Q8Easy
The abscissa (x-coordinate) of the point (7, −2) is:
−2
7
5
9
Hint: Abscissa = x-coordinate = the first number in the ordered pair. So for (7, −2), the abscissa is 7.
Medium 2 marks each
Q9Medium
A point lies on the negative x-axis at a distance of 5 units from the origin. Its coordinates are:
(5, 0)
(−5, 0)
(0, 5)
(0, −5)
Hint: Points on the x-axis have y = 0. "Negative x-axis" means x < 0. Distance 5 from origin → x = −5. So the point is (−5, 0).
Q10Medium
The point P(a, b) lies in Quadrant III. Which of the following must be true?
a > 0, b > 0
a < 0, b > 0
a < 0, b < 0
a > 0, b < 0
Hint: Quadrant III is the bottom-left region. Both x and y are negative there: (−,−).
Q11Medium
Which of the following points lies on the y-axis?
(3, 0)
(0, −4)
(2, 2)
(−1, 0)
Hint: A point lies on the y-axis when its x-coordinate is 0. Check: A has x=3, B has x=0 ✓, C has x=2, D has x=−1. Answer is B.
Q12Medium
The perpendicular distance of the point (3, −4) from the x-axis is:
3
4
7
5
Hint: The perpendicular distance from the x-axis = |y-coordinate| = |−4| = 4. (Distance from y-axis = |x| = 3.)
Q13Medium
If A = (−2, 3), in which quadrant does the reflection of A across the x-axis lie?
Quadrant I
Quadrant II
Quadrant III
Quadrant IV
Hint: Reflection across the x-axis flips the y-coordinate: (−2, 3) → (−2, −3). Now x < 0, y < 0 → Quadrant III.
Q14Medium
The set of all points (x, y) where x = y lies on:
The x-axis
The y-axis
A diagonal line through Q I and Q III
Only the origin
Hint: When x = y, every point like (1,1), (2,2), (−3,−3) satisfies it. These form a diagonal line through the origin passing through Q I and Q III.
Hard 3 marks each
Q15Hard
The vertices of a triangle are A(0, 0), B(4, 0) and C(0, 3). What is the area of the triangle?
3 sq. units
6 sq. units
12 sq. units
7 sq. units
Hint: AB lies along the x-axis (base = 4), C is directly above A (height = 3). Area = ½ × base × height = ½ × 4 × 3 = 6 sq. units.
Q16Hard
A point P is equidistant from both axes and lies in Quadrant II. If its distance from each axis is 4 units, what are its coordinates?
(4, 4)
(−4, −4)
(−4, 4)
(4, −4)
Hint: Distance from y-axis = |x| = 4, distance from x-axis = |y| = 4. In Quadrant II, x < 0 and y > 0, so x = −4, y = 4 → (−4, 4).
Q17Hard
Points A(1, 2) and B(1, −2) are plotted. The line segment AB is parallel to which axis?
The x-axis
The y-axis
Both axes
Neither axis
Hint: A and B have the same x-coordinate (x = 1). A vertical segment (x = constant) is parallel to the y-axis. Length AB = |2−(−2)| = 4 units.
Q18Hard
The four points A(2, 3), B(−2, 3), C(−2, −3), D(2, −3) form a rectangle. What is its area?
12 sq. units
24 sq. units
16 sq. units
8 sq. units
Hint: Width = 2−(−2) = 4 units. Height = 3−(−3) = 6 units. Area = 4 × 6 = 24 sq. units.
Q19Hard
When a point P(a, b) is reflected across the y-axis, what are the coordinates of its image P'?
(−a, b)
(a, −b)
(−a, −b)
(b, a)
Hint: Reflection across the y-axis flips the x-coordinate and keeps y the same: (a, b) → (−a, b). Example: (3, 5) → (−3, 5).
Q20Hard
A right triangle has vertices at (0, 0), (3, 0) and (0, 4). In which part of the plane does it lie, and what is the length of its hypotenuse?
Positive x and y axes / Q I; hypotenuse = 5 units
Positive x and y axes / Q I; hypotenuse = 7 units
Quadrant II; hypotenuse = 5 units
Positive x and y axes / Q I; hypotenuse = 3.5 units
Hint: All three vertices have non-negative coordinates — on the positive axes and origin. Hypotenuse = distance from (3,0) to (0,4) = √(3²+4²) = √25 = 5 units (3-4-5 Pythagorean triple).
