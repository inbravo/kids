(−3, 5)
(3, 5)

Hint: Reflection across the x-axis keeps x the same and flips y: (3, −5) → (3, 5). The point moves to the other side of the x-axis.

x < 0, y > 0

Hint: Quadrant IV is the bottom-right region — x is positive, y is negative: (+, −).

Q4Easy
The point (−4, 0) lies on the:
y-axis
x-axis
Quadrant II
Quadrant III
Hint: y = 0 means the point is on the x-axis. It is on the negative part of the x-axis (x = −4), but still on the x-axis — not in any quadrant.
Q5Easy
The "ordinate" of a point means its:
x-coordinate
y-coordinate
Distance from origin
Quadrant number
Hint: Ordinate = y-coordinate (second number). Abscissa = x-coordinate (first number). Together they form the ordered pair (abscissa, ordinate).
Q6Easy
A point has ordinate 0. It lies on the:
y-axis
x-axis
Origin only
Cannot be determined
Hint: Ordinate = y-coordinate = 0. Any point with y = 0 lies on the x-axis, e.g. (3, 0), (−5, 0), (0, 0).

2

Points A(2, 3) and B(2, −3) are plotted. What is the length of segment AB?
3 units
5 units
6 units
4 units
Hint: A and B have the same x-coordinate, so AB is vertical. Length = |y₁ − y₂| = |3 − (−3)| = 6 units.
Q10Medium

(0, 4)
(4, −4)
(−4, 0)

Hint: The three given vertices form three corners of a square with side 4. The missing corner must be at (0, 4) to complete the square in Quadrant I.
Q11Medium
What are the coordinates of the midpoint of the segment joining A(−2, 4) and B(4, −2)?
(1, 1)
(2, 2)
(−1, −1)
(1, −1)
Hint: Midpoint = ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2) = ((−2+4)/2, (4+(−2))/2) = (2/2, 2/2) = (1, 1).
Q12Medium
Point P(3, −4) is first reflected across the x-axis, then across the y-axis. What are the final coordinates?
(−3, 4)
(3, 4)
(−3, −4)
(3, −4)
Hint: Step 1 — reflect across x-axis (flip y): (3, −4) → (3, 4). Step 2 — reflect across y-axis (flip x): (3, 4) → (−3, 4).
Q13Medium
The points (1, 0), (0, 1) and (−1, 0) are all equidistant from which point?
(0, 1)
The origin (0, 0)
The x-axis
The y-axis
Hint: Distance from origin: √(1²+0²) = 1, √(0²+1²) = 1, √((−1)²+0²) = 1. All three are exactly 1 unit from the origin.
Q14Medium
The area of the triangle with vertices (0, 0), (6, 0) and (0, 4) is:
10 sq. units
12 sq. units
24 sq. units
6 sq. units
Hint: Base along x-axis = 6, height along y-axis = 4. Area = ½ × 6 × 4 = 12 sq. units.

Q15Hard
The points A(−1, 0), B(3, 0) and C(3, 4) form a right triangle. What is the length of the hypotenuse AC?
4 units
4√2 units
√32 units (same as 4√2)
√(16+16) = 4√2 units
Hint: AC = √((3−(−1))² + (4−0)²) = √(4²+4²) = √(16+16) = √32 = 4√2 units. Options B, C, D are all equivalent — B is the simplest form.
Q16Hard
A rectangle has vertices A(0, 0), B(4, 0), C(4, 3), D(0, 3). What type of quadrilateral is it and what is the length of diagonal AC?
Square; diagonal = 5 units
Rectangle; diagonal = 5 units
Rectangle; diagonal = 7 units
Parallelogram; diagonal = 5 units
Hint: Length = 4, width = 3 → not a square, so it is a rectangle. Diagonal AC = √(4²+3²) = √(16+9) = √25 = 5 units (3-4-5 triple).
Q17Hard
A point P(x, y) satisfies both x + y = 5 and x − y = 1. In which quadrant does P lie?
Quadrant I
Quadrant II
Quadrant III
Quadrant IV
Hint: Add the two equations: 2x = 6 → x = 3. Substitute: 3 + y = 5 → y = 2. P = (3, 2) — both positive → Quadrant I.
Q18Hard
The vertices of triangle PQR are P(0, 4), Q(−3, 0) and R(3, 0). What is the area of the triangle?
6 sq. units
8 sq. units
12 sq. units
24 sq. units
Hint: Base QR lies on the x-axis: length = 3−(−3) = 6. Height = perpendicular distance from P(0,4) to x-axis = 4. Area = ½ × 6 × 4 = 12 sq. units.
Q19Hard
A rectangle ABCD has A(−3, 2), B(3, 2), C(3, −2), D(−3, −2). What is its perimeter?
10 units
20 units
24 units
12 units
Hint: Length AB = 3−(−3) = 6. Width BC = 2−(−2) = 4. Perimeter = 2 × (6 + 4) = 20 units.
Q20Hard

(3, 4) only
(4, 3) only
(5, 0) only
All three: (3, 4), (4, 3) and (5, 0)
